Prototype App Built on Open Data

This is a prototype application built by DataLA -- the initiative to open up data from public institutions in Los Angeles and make it available and useful to citizens. This app was built by pulling data from the County and redeploying technology built by the City of Chicago. The Flu Shot app has been redeployed by many different cities, showing the opportunity for open data and reusable code to solve common problems. The code behind this web application is free, open, and under a MIT License. Feel free to send us comments.


Welcome to LA's Flu Shot Finder

This is a prototype application, designed to showcase the usefulness of open data.The City of Los Angeles does not commit to the accuracy of the data.

The County of Los Angeles runs a county-wide program to help you and your family and friends get a flu shot in this season; they are free and available across the region at various locations for the next month. Built on open data, this site helps you find the most convenient option.

If your device has GPS, you may click on the Find Me button to see locations near you. You may enter your address and click on Go to do the same. Click on All, Next 7 Days, or On A Day of the week to highlight locations available on a certain day.
